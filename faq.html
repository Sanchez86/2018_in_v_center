What is called the “underlying financial asset”?

The underlying asset is the financial instrument (such as stocks, futures, commodities, currency or indices) on which a derivative's price is based. The derivative`s price is correlated (directly or inversely) to the price of the underlying asset.There are four major markets that most common assets refer to:

  • Currencies (USD/JPY, USD/CHF, etc.)
  • Commodities (Gold, Oil, Sugar, etc.)
  • Stocks (SNAP Inc., Facebook Inc., Yahoo Inc, etc.)
  • Indices (Dow Jones, NASDAQ Composite, The Barclays Capital U.S. Aggregate Bond Index, S&P 500, etc.)

What is a CFD?

CFDs are tradable contracts between a client and a broker, who are exchanging the difference in the current value of a share, currency, commodity or index and its value at the contract’s end. CFDs provide investors with the all the benefits of owning a security without actually owning it

What kind of trading order is stop-loss?

A stop-loss order is a trader`s useful tool used in almost every trading strategy. This order is considered as an exit plan to limit the risk of a trade. The order is placed through a broker to close an active trade once the price of an asset hit a certain level of loss. For example, you bought 100 stocks at the price of $10 and expect it to reach $12 later this month, but you are not willing to suffer serious losses in case it does not, so you put a stop-loss order at 9$. If the price for the stock suddenly declines, your order will be fulfilled and your position will be automatically closed, saving you from a possibly greater loss. Note that you should use a stop-loss order wisely, and at your own discretion.

What payment methods are available to withdraw funds from my account?
