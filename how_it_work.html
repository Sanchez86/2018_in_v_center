CFD (a Contract for Difference)
is a form of trading that lets market participants (buyers and sellers) speculate on the price movement of a whole host of financial markets such as indices, shares, currencies, commodities and bonds without owning them and regardless of whether prices are rising or falling.

If you think the price of certain asset will go up, you open a “Buy” position and your profits will rise in line with any increase in that price. If you expect the price to go down, you open a “Sell” position to profit from the price decline.

Choose the Buy option if you expect the price of an asset to rise or the Sell option if you sure it is going to decrease.

As soon as you have followed the previous steps, you can press on Open Trade and the window with the extended information about this trade will show up. At the lower section of the window you can add additional options to your trade - Stop Loss and Take Profit.

Take Profit is designed to close a profitable trade when the price of an asset reaches the desired target. Just choose the anticipated price level. Stop Loss can help you to protect your account. Add it to your trade and there will be no need to check the prices every 10 minutes.

You can close your trades at any time. The trade is closed as soon as you click the button. You can also find information on your closed trades in the Trade History tab.

If you are going to trade an asset at the value different from the market price you should select the Order tab.
