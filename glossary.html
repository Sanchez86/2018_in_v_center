Account - A traditional personal bank account that is used for holding cash and/or securities and is administered by an investement dealer whose services are used by the client to implement his/her trading strategy.
Access Codes - Client`s credentials or passwords provided by the broker, for the client to gain access to the financial institution`s website.
Amount for Withdrawal - a certain (minimum)amount of money that is available to withdraw. The actual minimum amounts are $100 or $250 for a wire transfer.
Asset/Underlying Asset - a financial instrument (currencies, futures, stocks, commodities or indices), that is the base asset.
Asset Sub-categories - Currencies - EUR/USD, GBP/USD, CHF/USD.
Commodities - Wheat, Gold, Oil, Coffee.
Stocks - Yahoo, Intel, Apple, etc.
Indices - Nasdaq, Dow 30, S&P 500, etc.
Balance – is the amount of money in the client`s personal trading account. The account balance is always the net amount after factoring in profits & losses.
Bearish – is the definition used to describe the market`s negative behavior. It is the situation when the majority of participants are sellers and the prices are going down.
Bond – is a fixed-income security, a loan from a company or government. Bonds are similar to stocks, however while stockholders hold an equity stake in a company, bondholders hold only a creditors stake in a company.
Bullish – Another definition to express the market`s general mood. The opposite to the term “bearish”. When the market is bullish, the majority of buyers are buying and the prices are rising.
CFD – the difference between where a trade is entered and exited. A CFD is a tradable instrument that mirrors the movements of the asset underlying it. It allows for profits or losses to be realized when the underlying asset moves in relation to the position taken, but the actual underlying asset is never owned. Trading CFDs has several major advantages, and these have increased the popularity of the instruments over the last several years.
Closed Position - A completed investment in which both a buy and sell order has been executed, resulting in either a profit or a loss.
Collateral Funds - in margin trading, the securities in a brokerage account act as collateral in case of a margin call. Additional money added to a trader`s account makes it able to maintain open positions and/or execute transactions.
Contract – is a fixed amount of an asset that forms a trading unit.
Cryptocurrency - is a digital currency that uses cryptography for security. It has swiftly established itself as a highly profitable financial instrument.The defining feature of a cryptocurrency is that it is not issued by any central authority, which makes it theoretically immune to government interference or manipulation.
Economic Indicator – economic statistics that are analyzed to forecast future market trends and the general direction of a country`s, or other assets economies.
Entry Rate/Strike Price - is the price at which an investor buys an investment. Opening a trade at a good strike rate is a start of a successful trade. The entry rate is usually a part of a predetermined trading strategy for minimizing investment risk and removing emotion from trading decisions.
Equity – stands for funds in a trader`s trading account including gained profits and accumulated losses for all opened positions.
Exposure Coverage – is the amount that can be lost in an investment. Knowing and understanding financial exposure, which is just another name for risk, is a crucial part of the investment process.
Forex/Foreign Exchange - a trading system that is based on simultaneous purchase of one currency and selling of another. These kind of transactions are represented on the global market as the Forex market.
Fundamentals – include the qualitative and quantitative information that affects the economic health and the financial valuation of a company, security or currency.
Fundamental Analysis – One of two major methods of asset analysis based on external events and influences, as well as the financial statements on the asset itself. It is used by traders to make decisions on different assets by measuring the economic, financial and market conditions that can affect its price.
GTC (Good-Till-Cancelled) Order - a Buy or a Sell order at a pre-set price that remains in effect until the investor cancels the order or the trade is completed.
Hedge – is a strategy intended to protect an investment or portfolio against loss. It usually involves buying securities that moves in the opposite direction than the asset being invested in.
If Done Order – an order that consists of two-steps. The first order is a limit, which is activated once the needed price is reached. The second order is dormant, activated once the first order has been executed. Such orders are left in an expectation of price fluctuations, but without the need to monitor the market 24-hours a day, to get the desired price.
Introducing Broker - a person or business that provides investing advice or counsel to an investor, but does not actually handle transactions. Generally speaking, introducing brokers make recommendations while delegating the task of executing trades to someone at the same or a different firm who operates on a trading floor. The term most often applies to brokers in the futures market.
IPO – An Initial Public Offering is the first time that a private company is offered to the public.
Leading Indicators – are certain statistics that are beyond recent events. They predict the future financial or economic trends, in the long term.
Leverage - is the investment strategy based on usage of borrowed money to increase a trader`s speculative capacity.
Limit Order – an order that allows to set a certain buy or sell price for an asset and open trades automatically as soon as that price is reached.
Long Position – stands for the purchase of a certain asset at a particular price with the expectation of the bullish market.
Maintenance Margin – is a certain limit after which an investor can receive a margin call from the broker. A trader has to make sure that he has enough equity to cover his open positions.
Margin Call – is a call from a trader`s broker to deposit additional funds so that the trading account is brought up to the minimum required maintenance margin.
Margin Usage – refers to the purchase of an asset where the buyer pays only a percentage of the asset's value and borrows the rest from a bank or broker. The broker acts as a lender, and he uses the funds in the securities account as collateral on the loan's balance.
Market Maker – is an institution or a brokerage company that is ready at any moment of the trading day with a firm ask and bid price.
Market Order – is an instant order execution at the current market price; a market order guarantees execution, and it also usually has low commissions due to the minimum work that a broker has to perform.
Net Exposure – is the percentage difference between a trader`s long and short exposure. It equals the sum of the of the long positions less the least value of the sum of the of short positions It is a measure of to the extent to which a trader`s trading account is exposed to market fluctuations.
Open Order – is a Buy or a Sell order that remains active until either it is canceled or executed.
Open Position – is the trade that is still able to bring profit or loss. As soon as the position is closed, all gains and losses are realised and the trade no longer exists.
Open P&L – Profits and Losses, shows a trader`s gains or losses in his open trades, at any moment.
Order Execution – the service that ensures that a trader`s order is executed with the best available price.
Overnight Position – is a trading position that remains open at the end of the trading day. The end of the forex trading day is 5.00 PM EST.
Pair – a traded currency quotation. The value of a currency is a rate which is found by making a comparison with another currency.
PIP (Point in Percentage) – is a set standard unit and the smallest amount by which a currency quote can change.
Portfolio – is the combination of securities held by an investor. This combination represents a variety of asset classes and/or other types of investments.
Position – is an investment that is owned (a long position) or borrowed and then sold (a short position) by a trader.
Profit – is the realized financial gain; the exact amount of revenue received when closing an open position.
Quote – a price at which the last security or commodity was traded, it is the latest price on which both a buyer and a seller agreed and at which an amount of the security was transacted.
Range – A trading range is the spread between the high and low prices traded during a specific period of time. The range displays an asset`s price fluctuations.
Risk – the chance of an investment's actual return that will differ from the expected return. Risk includes the possibility of losing some or all of the original investment.
Risk Management – the process of determining and evaluating financial risks and selecting trading methods to adapt to risk exposures
Rollover – is what happens at end of trading day, when all open positions are rolled over to the next business day.
Sell – stands for the action of liquidating an open long position.
Short Position – is the type of trading when a trader sells an asset with the expectation of the price to decrease over some period of time.
Spread – is the difference between the bid and the ask price of an asset.
Stop Entry Order – is an order to buy a security as soons as its price breaks a certain level, ensuring a higher chance of achieving a previously determined entry price, locking in a trader`s profit.
Stop Loss Order – is an order to sell a security as soon as it falls to a specific price. This particular type of order is designed to avoid huge losses.
Swap – refers to an exchange of one financial instrument for another between the parties concerned. This exchange takes place at a predetermined time, as specified in the contract.
T/P – This is an order, as popular as a stop-loss, and used by traders to set a certain rate or number of pips from the current price level where their current positions are to be closed for profit.
Trade Size – simply, is a amount of a an asset trading.
Trading Hours – the financial market`s working hours, its business day, when it is possible to perform trades.
Trading Platform – is a software provided by a broker in order to buy and sell assets in the market, receiveing market information as it happens, considers rules or sets of criteria that a trader sets up, and processes trades based on those rules and criteria specifications.
Trend – is the rising of falling direction of a market, its prices or economy in general, over time.
Turnover – is the volume or value of financial assets traded on the market during a trading day, month, year or any chosen period of time.
